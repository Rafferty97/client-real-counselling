E-Counselling

E-Counselling is very exciting because university reports are showing that it is proving to be as good as, and at times even more successful than traditional face-to-face counselling. The ‘paper trail’ is largely responsible for this success. Clients can use the printed word to re-read and focus on what was presented to them throughout the counselling session. This is an advantage because many people feel anxious particularly at the first counselling session and they can easily forget important points.

Advantages of E-Counselling

  • Convenience — In today’s busy world E-Counselling is becoming very popular because it’s so convenient. You can have counselling in your lunch hour or anywhere that you have Wi-Fi.
  • Autonomy — The autonomous person can be even more self-governing and independent as you can choose when you are not bound to rigid appointment times and face to face communications.
  • Accessibility — Many people are house bound either because of illness, disability or because they have young children. E-Counselling is proving to be such a help for these clients because the client does not have to leave their home.
  • Anonymity — Some people feel more relaxed without the ‘face to face’ communication of a traditional counselling session. This is an excellent way of getting support if you are a terribly shy person or a war veteran for instance. E-Counselling is far less confrontational!
